About Aditya Chilka

Aditya Chilka is a tech entrepreneur, AI innovator, product builder, and business leader based in Hyderabad, India. He is the Founder and CEO of Rapid Circuitry (legal name: Caropet Technologies Pvt Ltd), an embedded hardware company founded in 2020. He holds a Bachelor of Engineering in Electronics & Communication from Osmania University and a Master of Science in Supply Chain & Logistics Management from Curtin University, Australia. He is an Ashoka Changemaker with over 10 years of experience building hardware products and embedded systems.

What does Rapid Circuitry do?

Rapid Circuitry offers six core services: Custom PCB Design, Embedded Firmware Development (STM32, ESP32, nRF52), IoT Product Development, Medical Device Engineering, GPS/LoRa Asset Tracking, and Edge AI Systems. The company has delivered over 500 projects with a 99.8% first-pass PCB yield and an average turnaround of 60 days from idea to production-ready hardware. They serve clients worldwide across medical devices, industrial IoT, agriculture, logistics, and consumer electronics.

How I think

THE THINKER

First principles, always

I don't start with what exists — I start with what should exist. Every project begins with a question: what's the actual constraint? Not the assumed one, the real one. Strip away convention, challenge the datasheet, rethink the architecture. The best solutions come from thinking deeper, not faster.

THE INNOVATOR

AI is the new soldering iron

I'm building AI tools that compress weeks of PCB layout into hours. Trace routing, component placement, design rule checks — machines handle the grunt work so engineers focus on architecture. This isn't incremental improvement. It's rethinking how hardware gets designed from the ground up.

THE PROBLEM SOLVER

Own the full stack

Real problems don't respect boundaries. A PCB without firmware is copper. Firmware without connectivity is isolated. I solve end-to-end — sensor to cloud, edge to dashboard, device to fleet. When you own every layer, you can fix anything. That's how every project ships without finger-pointing.

THE ENGINEER

Build for the real world

Wearable monitors catching cardiac events in rural clinics. LoRa sensors cutting water waste on farms. Radar tracking vitals without touching skin. I design for 45°C warehouses, not air-conditioned labs. Production-first. DFM-baked. Every trace reviewed by hand. The engineer in me refuses to ship anything I wouldn't trust with my own life.
